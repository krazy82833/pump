Selection Guide Jul 2026

How to Choose a Micro Diaphragm Air Pump for Medical Devices

Medical equipment buyers often search for a micro diaphragm air pump that is compact, oil-free, low noise, and stable under repeated duty cycles.

Read article

A suitable medical micro air pump should be selected by flow rate, vacuum level, pressure, voltage, noise, lifetime, and installation space. For diagnostic equipment, therapy devices, sampling instruments, and compact suction modules, engineers usually need a pump that can maintain consistent airflow without oil contamination.

For early screening, BD-01 to BD-04 diaphragm air pump platforms cover compact air and vacuum needs from low-flow sampling to stronger suction. The final model should be confirmed with real tubing length, filter resistance, valve loss, duty cycle, and ambient temperature.

  • Key considerations: airflow, vacuum, pressure, noise, voltage, life test, and material compatibility.
  • OEM advice: test the full air path instead of testing the pump alone.
Liquid Pump Jul 2026

Diaphragm Liquid Pump Selection for Precision Dispensing and Water Purifiers

For liquid dispensing, water purifier modules, and small filling systems, a self-priming diaphragm liquid pump must match flow, pressure, medium, and diaphragm material.

Read article

A diaphragm liquid pump is often chosen when equipment needs clean transfer, self-priming ability, compact size, and reliable isolation between motor and fluid. Common applications include water purifiers, beverage dispensing, cleaning systems, laboratory dosing, and beauty devices.

When selecting a pump, confirm the liquid viscosity, chemical properties, inlet height, outlet restriction, required flow rate, and whether the pump must run dry during startup. EPDM and FKM diaphragm options can be considered depending on the medium.

  • Key considerations: self-priming height, pressure, flow, liquid compatibility, and leakage control.
  • OEM advice: evaluate check valves, tubing diameter, and filter pressure drop together.
Pressure Systems Jul 2026

Piston Air Pump vs Mini Compressor: Pressure, Flow, and Duty Cycle

Piston air pumps and mini compressors both support high-pressure air systems, but they are not interchangeable in every OEM design.

Read article

A piston air pump is usually selected when the product needs higher pressure, stable output, and stronger vacuum or compression performance than a small diaphragm pump. A mini compressor is better for higher air volume, stronger pressure bands, and equipment that needs a compact compressed-air source.

BD-07, BD-79, and BD-08 style platforms should be evaluated by pressure, flow, power, heat rise, duty cycle, noise, and vibration. For robotics, rehabilitation equipment, cleaning systems, and 3D printing accessories, the load profile matters as much as the rated pressure.

  • Key considerations: peak pressure, continuous pressure, airflow, power, and thermal behavior.
  • OEM advice: include pressure relief, shock absorption, and noise control in the module design.
OEM Integration Jul 2026

Reducing Noise and Vibration in OEM Micro Pump Modules

Low noise is not only a pump specification. It depends on motor type, mounting structure, tubing, housing resonance, and air path design.

Read article

Many buyers ask for a low-noise micro pump, but real device noise is created by the whole system. Rigid mounting, sharp tubing bends, restrictive filters, resonance inside a plastic enclosure, and unstable voltage can all increase sound and vibration.

For OEM modules, use soft mounting points, suitable shock absorbers, silencers, stable PWM control, and properly sized tubing. Test the final product in its real enclosure, not only on an open bench.

  • Key considerations: motor speed, mounting, inlet/outlet resistance, enclosure resonance, and duty cycle.
  • OEM advice: combine pump selection with accessory design for better acoustic results.
Accessories Jul 2026

Pump Accessories That Improve Reliability: Valves, Tubing, Filters, and Silencers

A good pump module is more than a pump. Accessories control flow stability, contamination risk, startup load, noise, and maintenance life.

Read article

Check valves, filters, silencers, tubing, fittings, flow meters, and solenoid valves can decide whether a micro pump system works reliably in production. A mismatched accessory can reduce flow, increase noise, overload the motor, or create leakage risk.

When designing an OEM fluid control module, define the medium, temperature, pressure, tubing route, connector size, filtration needs, and control method. JSG can help match pumps with accessories for air, vacuum, and liquid systems.

  • Key considerations: sealing, pressure loss, material compatibility, flow feedback, and maintenance.
  • OEM advice: validate the complete pump plus accessory module before mass production.
